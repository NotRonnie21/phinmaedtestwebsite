A Brief History of the Schools of the PHINMA Education Network

1950

Araullo University started operations as Araullo Law School. The school was named after Manuel G. Araullo, the second Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines. It initially had twenty-seven students attending class in Cabanatuan Puericulture Center Building in Paco Roman Street.

1951

Araullo Law School transferred to M. H. Del Pilar Street.

1961

Araullo Law School became a college. It was renamed as Araullo Lyceum.

1963

Araullo Lyceum was cited as the Most Outstanding Institution in Central Luzon by the League of Provincial Governors. Since then, the school has garnered other prestigious awards and recognitions.

1969

Araullo Lyceum acquired the four-hectare property at Barangay Bitas, along the Maharlika Highway. On this property is where all the buildings, structures, and facilities of Araullo University now stand.

1984

Araullo Lyceum became a university. It was renamed as Pamantasan ng Araullo (Araullo University).

2004

Pamantasan ng Araullo was acquired by PHINMA. The school is now known as Araullo University - PHINMA Education Network.

1948

Cagayan de Oro College started operations as Parent-Teacher College. Initially, the school offered classes in the elementary and secondary levels, and courses in Education, Liberal Arts, Secretarial, and Commerce in the tertiary level.

1955

Parent-Teacher College transferred to Barangay Carmen.

1967

Parent-Teacher College was renamed as Cagayan de Oro College.

1981

Cagayan de Oro College opened its graduate school. It began offering the program for Master of Arts in Teaching Social Studies.

1988

Cagayan de Oro College began offering technical-vocational courses. It offered Mechanical Technology, Electrical Technology, and Electronics Technology.

1991

Cagayan de Oro College began offering a ladderized curriculum for Bachelor of Science in Architecture.

1998

Cagayan de Oro College began offering its first doctorate degree: Ph.D. in Educational Administration and Supervision.

2003

The Philippine Association of Colleges and University – Commission on Accreditation (PACU-CCA) awards Cagayan de Oro College for having the third highest number of accredited programs.

2004

The Philippine Association of Colleges and University-Commission on Accreditation (PACU-CCA) awards Cagayan de Oro College for having the second highest number of accredited programs.

2005

Cagayan de Oro College was acquired by PHINMA. The school is now known as Cagayan de Oro College – PHINMA Education Network.

University of Pangasinan

1925

University of Pangasinan started operations as Dagupan Institute. It initially offered elementary, secondary, and vocational courses.

1941

Dagupan Institute was renamed as Dagupan Junior Colleges. It started offering a Bachelor's degree in Education.

1950

Dagupan Junior Colleges was renamed as Dagupan Colleges and started offering more degree programs.

1968

Dagupan Colleges is renamed as University of Pangasinan.

1998

The Philippine Regulatory Commission rated University of Pangasinan as the 4th Best Performing School in Midwifery.

2000

The Philippine Regulatory Commission rated University of Pangasinan as the 5th Best Performing School in Architecture.

2009

University of Pangasinan was acquired by PHINMA. The school is now known as University of Pangasinan – PHINMA Education Network.

1947

University of Iloilo started operations as Iloilo City Colleges. The first classes were held in Quonset huts with nipa roofs, sawali and bamboo walls, and mud floors. There were two campuses: the Iloilo Unit and the La Paz Unit.

1963

Iloilo City Colleges opened its Graduate School for the professional growth of teachers and administrators.

1966

Iloilo City Colleges opened the College of Criminology in the main campus. Simultaneously, it opened its College of Agriculture at Ungka, Pavia.

1968

Iloilo City Colleges was granted university status. As University of Iloilo, it became the 32nd university of the Philippines.

1972

University of Iloilo purchased the Philippine Women's College in Jaro. The school was converted into the Don Benito Lopez Hospital.

1993

A certificate of recognition was awarded to the College of Criminology for its outstanding performance in the pursuit of quality education.

1998

The University of Iloilo entered into a contract with Net Curriculo Incorporated to integrate technology in classroom instruction.

2009

University of Iloilo was acquired by PHINMA. The school is now known as University of Iloilo - PHINMA Education Network.

Academic Policies

A. Grading System B. Release of Grades C. Incomplete Grade D. Academic Probation E. Class Attendance F. Promotion and Retention

The grading system will be provided to the students by their respective teachers through the syllabus. If understanding of the system is not clear, it is the responsibility of the student to seek clarification from his/her teacher, program head or dean.


Grades are given out in numerical format. A student who has passed the subject will receive any of the following grodes: 1.00, 1.25, 1.50, 1.75, 2.00, 2.25, 2:50, 2.75, or 3.00. The grade of 1.00 is the highest possible grade that a student may get. A student who has failed the subject will receive a grade of 5.00.


A student may also get a grade other than those specified earlier. These grades may be given for various reasons such as, but not limited to: incompleteness of submitted requirements, withdrawal from a subject-with permission, withdrawal from a subject-without permission, failure due to absences, no examination because the student was not able to obtain an exam permit.


Final grades will be released by the Office of the Registrar. Other term exams will be released by the faculty.

A student who gets on incomplete grade has two consecutive terms The Dean/Director must have approved the granting of the incomplete grade Failure to get a passing grede for an incomplete grade after two terms has elapsed means that the student will get a failing mark in the subject concerned, The corresponding completion fee shall be applied. to complete.

A student who incurs falling grades in three (3) subjects of his academic lood in a semester wiil be accepted on probation the following semester and the student will only be allowed to carry a maximum load of 18 units. Should the student lail in two (2) subjects during the probationary period, a maximum load of 12 units shall be enforced. The student may assume a regular load only when the mentioned 12 units have been passed.

1. Every student is required to attend his classes regularly and punctually. 6. No excuse for an absentee or absences will be considered valid unless the student con show that his absences were unavoidable or necessary.


7. In case of an absence due to sickness, the student must present a written note from parent/guardian or certificate from an attending physicion stating the nature of the sickness

Student Services

A. Library The Library serves as a venue for study and research. To continuously meet the needs of the students, its collections are regularly updated. For clarification on library policies, you may consult with the Librarian.
B. Computer and Internet Access Like the Library, the Computer Labs serve as venues for study and research. Students with access to these services may use the Computer Labs to practice computer skills or conduct research on the internet. For clarification on computer lab policies, you may consult with the IT Officer.
C. Cafeteria Students may go to the Cafeteria to buy or eat their food. Students may also buy in nearby food stalls within the campus.
D. Business Center The Business Center serves as a one-stop venue wherein students may buy textbooks, uniforms, and other school-related materials.
E. Guidance and Counseling The Guidance and Counseling Office serves as a venue where students may ask for career guidance and counseling. It also interprets a student’s performance in aptitude or psychological tests that a student has taken. The office also counsels students who have been having difficulties in their academics or in following the Student Code of Conduct.
F. Health The School offers medical and dental services to students. These services include daily consultation and physical examination. Minor surgical procedures (with minimal charge) and other emergency or first aid treatments are also available to students. Initial dosage of medicines will be given for simple ailments like headache, fever, cough and colds, and diarrhea. A medical certificate will be issued and given to the student after he has been examined and seen by the physician.
G. Campus Ministry The Campus Ministry Office is responsible for providing opportunities for the spiritual growth of the students and the school community in general. It is tasked to maintain an interfaith council that will represent the different faiths in the School.
H. Financial Aid The School provides scholarships to deserving students who need financial aid to continue their studies. Students may consult with the Center for Student Development and Leadership for details on how to apply for a scholarship.
I. Personal Accident Insurance Students may claim insurance if they had personal accidents. High-risk activities leading to personal accidents are not covered by this service. For more information on the personal accident insurance policy, students may consult with the Center for Student Development and Leadership.
J. Security The School is committed to maintaining a campus that is safe and conducive to learning. It assigns security personnel and student marshals who help maintain the security of the School. Students are encouraged to report to any security personnel any person who represents a threat to the safety of people and/or property in the campus.
K. Recognized Student Organizations The School encourages the establishment, growth, and active participation of student organizations. Students may visit the Center for Student Development and Leadership for details on establishing, running, or joining student organizations.

Appendix 1: About the PHINMA Group

How it all began

The PHINMA story began in 1956 and it's a kind of story that will be told and retold as a fitting tribute to the enterprising men who had high hopes for the Filipino and the national economy. Their names belong to an honor list of Who's Who in Philippine business—Filamon C. Rodriguez, Ramon V. del Rosario, Sr. and Ernesto O. Escoler.

These pioneers were accomplished professionals in their respective fields. They were young and ambitious. But they were different. They were visionaries and they set themselves apart by thinking big for the country and its people. They believed in developing Filipino entrepreneurship, in promoting the professional management skills of Filipino managers, in corporate social responsibility and community building.

With the establishment of PHINMA came the birth of a business organization that would give rise to a culture of excellence—one that would touch the lives of millions of Filipinos and contribute to the development of Filipino enterprise, as well as the upliftment of the average person's quality of life.

Milestones

1956

Ramon V. del Rosario, Sr., Filamon C. Rodriguez, and Ernesto O. Escoler form PHINMA, then the first Filipino-managed investment firm.
The new company purchased Cabu Portland Cement Company in Bocnota, La Union. It was renamed Bocnotan Cement Corporation (BCC) using the brand name Union Cement. Since then, BCC has become the foremost producer of cement in the Philippines, together with its subsidiary-sister plants.

1959

PHINMA established an oil refinery in Rosario, Cavite. Filoli's marketing strategy of "Filipino First" inspired a nationalistic sense of consumerism among Filipinos to purchase Filoli.

1963

PHINMA ventured into GI sheets manufacturing, through Bocnotan Consolidated Industries Incorporated, with its steel plant in Poro Point, La Union. This spawned a galvanizing plant known as Union Galvestrel Corporation.

1969

Trans-Asia Oil and Mineral Company was formed to undergo exploration in the northwest seas of Palawan. Success in this endeavor has snowballed into other energy projects such as coal mining and energy generation.

1971

PHINMA ventured into the paper and packaging business with the United Pulp and Paper Company (UPPC) in Calumpit, Bulacan. The plant produced sack Kraft paper, for use in the cement packaging business. UPPC has continuously upgraded their production capacity and has increased revenues significantly.

1984

Community Relations, COMREL, was formed to reach out to the communities where PHINMA plants are located, and to coordinate with local government units in order to improve Filipino livelihood. COMREL is PHINMA’s example of good corporate citizenship and community building.

1987

PHINMA Realty Corporation and FC Development Corp merged into a new company: PHINMA Property Holdings Corporation. With housing projects being prosperous, other businesses evolved including property management and sanitation.

1989

Through the efforts of Mr. Ramon del Rosario, Jr., PHINMA partnered with A. Soriano Co. and the Benguet Mining Corporation to launch the Asian Savings Bank into a commercial banking institution in 1989, later named Asian Bank in 1990.

1995

PHINMA constructed the Don Filemon C. Rodriguez Training Center in Tagaytay City under a spin-off company named PHINMA Group Development Foundation. The Training Center houses facilities for management as well as technical training over 2.7 hectares. It has 52-room trainees quarters for overnight sessions. The center is also open to non-PHINMA companies.

1996

PHINMA's corporate social responsibility program, which is run through PHINMA COMREL, fostered the communal spirit along with the individual’s sense of responsibility to the community, as well as promoted self-sufficiency in the immediate vicinity. For this, PHINMA received the Corporate Citizenship Excellence Award from the Philippine Business for Social Progress.

1997

Trans-Asia Power Generation Corporation, the joint venture project of Trans-Asia Oil and Hi-Cement Corporation, commissioned its 52 MW power plant in Bulacan.

1998

Bacnotan Consolidated Industries, Inc. (BCII) and Atlas Cement Corporation (ACC) entered into an agreement with Holderbank for a strategic partnership in the cement business of PHINMA.

1999

United Pulp and Paper Company, Inc. inaugurated its second paper mill amounting to ₱6 Billion, making it the biggest and most advanced paper manufacturing facility in the Philippines. This was a joint venture project between PHINMA and Thailand’s Siam Cement Group, the largest industrial conglomerate in Thailand.

2000

The three cement companies of PHINMA, namely Bacnotan Cement Corporation, Hi-Cement Corporation, and Davao Union Cement Corporation, merged into UNION Cement Corporation in an effort to integrate and improve coordination among the plants.

2001

PHINMA and other subsidiaries/sister companies moved into the new PHINMA Plaza in Rockwell Center, Makati.

2003

Siam Pulp and Paper Company, PHINMA’s strategic partner in the paper business, agreed to infuse new equity that completed the buy-out of United Pulp and Paper Company.

2004

PHINMA sold its investment in the Cement Business, marking the end of more than four decades of industrial primacy and the beginning of a new journey into the services sector. Also, in the same year, PHINMA and BCII acquired a controlling interest in Araullo University, its maiden investment in the Education sector.

2006

PHINMA celebrated its 50th year. As part of a five-university system goal, PHINMA acquired Cagayan de Oro College. Demonstrating its zeal in providing affordable housing, PHINMA acquired the Philippine franchise of Microtel Inns & Suites. Through its wholly owned subsidiary, PHINMA Property Holdings Corp. (PPHC), the company also became a leader in affordable medium-rise housing in Metro Manila with more than 4,000 condo units.

2007

PHINMA ventures into Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) with the following firms: PHINMA Finance and Accounting Outcomes Corporation (PHINMA F&A), and PHINMA Architecture and Engineering Outcomes Corporation (PHINMA A&E). PHINMA BPO companies provide offshore clientele with cost-effective and value-added offshore services.

2009

PHINMA adds two more schools to the PHINMA Education Network (PEN). These are University of Rangarban and University of Iloilo. PEN now has four schools in the network.
PHINMA also adds Toon City Animation, Inc. to its group of BPO companies. Toon City provides outsource animation production services to major international studios. As a 15-year old studio providing traditional, Flash and 3D CGI (computer generated imagery) animation production services, Toon City has excelled at producing Feduver Animation, Direct to Video (DTV), TV series, and short programs for major clients such as Woh Diang, Nickelodeon, Werner Brox, and Universal Studios.

Appendix 2: PHINMA Group's Mission

The PHINMA GROUP'S MISSION is to help build our Nation through competitive and well-managed business enterprises that enable Filipinos to attain a better quality of life. With effective management as our attractive edge, we aim to give Filipinos improved access to the essentials of a dignified life.

We will focus our ongoing enterprises and seek new opportunities in pursuit of our Mission. We will build not only affordable and decent homes, but also wholesome communities in urban areas. We will offer not only affordable high quality education, but also a brighter future for globally competitive Filipino professionals and workers. We will explore opportunities in the energy sector to offer not only reliable and affordable power, but also more productive lives – particularly to communities in greatest need. We will offer attractive investment opportunities and sound investment advice to encourage capital formation. We will aggressively seek opportunities primarily in the services sector that will allow us to address the basic needs of our society, while being globally competitive and generating attractive stakeholder values.

In pursuit of our Mission, we look to our tradition, our experience, our reputation and, above all, our people, as the principal factors that will enable us to achieve our lofty goals.

We believe that a well-qualified and competitively compensated work force, treated with trust and respect, will be a highly motivated and productive partner in the pursuit of our goals. We shall continue to conduct ourselves according to the highest ideals of competence, patriotism, integrity and professionalism. The PHINMA Group believes that the process of self-improvement and innovation, and the quest for excellence, are never-ending tasks.

The PHINMA Group shall forge strategic alliances with partners who share our values and concerns and who can provide capital, expertise, technology, market access and other strategic elements. As a general rule, the PHINMA Group shall invest in enterprises to the extent needed to support a continuing and meaningful management role.

In focusing on providing for the basic needs of the Filipino through well-managed enterprises, the PHINMA Group shall demonstrate that private business can mutually serve the aspirations of shareholders and the needs of society.

Appendix 3: PHINMA Today

Changes in the Philippine business climate and the global economy have led the PHINMA Group to embark on a new direction. From a conglomerate involved in heavy industry and manufacturing, particularly in cement and paper, the company now believes that it will best serve the nation's and its stockholders' interest by focusing on the Filipinos' basic needs.

With effective management as its principal resource, the company believes it can contribute high-quality but affordable education, decent and affordable homes, reliable and affordable electricity, and other goods and services that touch people's lives daily.

Today, the PHINMA Group is pursuing opportunities in energy, low-cost housing, education, and financial services, and is considering investments in service-oriented businesses. To illustrate, the company laid the foundation of its education business in 2004 with the acquisition of the 7,000-student Araullo University in Cabanatuan City. Its low-cost housing subsidiary, PHINMA Property Holdings, has become the largest developer of low-cost, medium-rise condominiums. Its affiliate TA Oil & Energy has constructed a power plant that will supply the energy requirements of Guimaras province in the Visayas.

These companies make up a huge reservoir of potential contribution that the country would require to sustain its growth program. With its capabilities in energy development, power generation, education, low-cost housing, financial and management services, the PHINMA Group is well on its way to becoming a major player in the country's thrust toward social upliftment and economic progress—a familiar role envisioned by its founders since its establishment in 1956.
